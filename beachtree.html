Beech Tree (Fagus sp.)

The beech tree (Fagus sp.) is a majestic deciduous tree known for its smooth gray bark, distinctive leaves, edible nuts (mast), and strong timber. It is a key species in many temperate forests.
USES: Highly valued for its hard, strong wood used in furniture, flooring, and small objects. The nuts are edible for wildlife and humans (often roasted). Traditionally, parts of the tree were used for medicinal purposes.
Health Benefits (Traditional): Beech tree bark and leaves possess astringent and antiseptic properties. Historically used in poultices for skin conditions or as a gargle for sore throats. Creosote derived from beechwood tar has disinfectant uses. Modern medicinal use is limited compared to other herbs.
Precautions: The nuts should be consumed in moderation as large quantities can be mildly toxic. Individuals allergic to beech pollen or other tree nuts should exercise caution. Consult healthcare providers before using any part for medicinal purposes.
