Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale)

Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) is a common flowering plant, often seen as a weed, recognizable by its bright yellow composite flowers and characteristic toothed leaves. Every part of the plant is edible and has medicinal uses.
USES: Leaves are used in salads or cooked as greens. Roots are roasted as a coffee substitute or used in herbal remedies. Flowers can be used for wine or jellies. Traditionally utilized for liver support, digestion aid, and as a diuretic.
Health Benefits: Rich in vitamins (A, C, K) and minerals. Supports liver and kidney function by promoting detoxification. Acts as a mild diuretic and laxative, aiding digestion and reducing water retention. Contains antioxidants.
Precautions: Generally safe, but individuals allergic to related plants (ragweed, daisies, chrysanthemums) may react. Can interact with certain medications (like diuretics or blood thinners). Consult a doctor if you have gallbladder issues before consuming large amounts.
