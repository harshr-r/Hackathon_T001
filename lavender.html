Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia, often English Lavender) is a highly aromatic flowering shrub belonging to the mint family. It's renowned for its beautiful purple flowers and distinct, calming fragrance.
USES: Widely used in aromatherapy (essential oil) for relaxation and sleep. Employed in perfumes, cosmetics, and culinary applications (flowers and leaves). Used in potpourri, sachets, and herbal pillows. Traditionally used for anxiety, headaches, and skin ailments.
Health Benefits: The essential oil is known for its calming, sedative effects, helping to reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality. Possesses antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties beneficial for minor burns, insect bites, and skin irritation. May help alleviate headaches.
Precautions: Essential oil should generally be diluted before applying to skin to avoid irritation. Internal consumption of large amounts or undiluted oil is not recommended. May cause drowsiness; use caution if operating machinery. Consult a doctor if pregnant, nursing, or taking sedative medications.
