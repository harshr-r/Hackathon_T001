Aloe Vera (Aloe vera)

Aloe vera is a succulent plant widely used for its gel, which promotes skin health, aids wound healing, and offers various medicinal benefits.
USES: Aloe vera is used for skin care (burns, hydration, irritation), wound healing, promoting digestion, and boosting overall health in traditional medicine and modern cosmetics.
Health Benefits: Known for its anti-inflammatory, moisturizing, and antimicrobial properties. Supports skin healing, improves digestion when taken internally (with caution), and boosts hydration. Rich in antioxidants.
Precautions: The latex (yellow substance under the skin) can cause stomach upset or cramps if ingested. Pregnant women and individuals with specific gastrointestinal issues or allergies to plants in the Liliaceae family should exercise caution or avoid internal use. Always consult a professional for internal consumption guidelines.
